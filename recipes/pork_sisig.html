Pork Sisig

A delicious chopped pork dish!

Pork Sisig is a flavorful and sizzling Filipino dish that has become a culinary sensation both in the Philippines and internationally. The dish typically features finely chopped and seasoned pork, often including parts like pig's face and ears, grilled or pan-fried until crispy. Served on a hotplate, it is commonly garnished with chopped onions, green chili peppers, and calamansi for a burst of citrusy flavor. The combination of crispy textures and bold, savory flavors makes Pork Sisig a beloved choice among food enthusiasts.

The origins of Sisig can be traced back to the culinary heritage of Pampanga, a province in the Philippines known for its rich and diverse cuisine. Initially conceived as a snack to complement sour flavors, the original Sisig was crafted using unripe fruits in a vinegar dressing. Over time, the dish evolved, and meat, particularly pork, became a prominent ingredient. The transformation of Sisig from a tangy snack to a savory pork delicacy reflects the adaptability and creativity within Filipino gastronomy.

  • 1 pound pork belly - sliced about half-inch thick
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 pieces chicken liver
  • 1 big red onion - finely chopped
  • ½ lemon or 3-4 kalamansi juice
  • 1-2 chili peppers - minced or ¼ tablespoon chili flakes
  • ½ tablespoon soy sauce
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground black pepper
  1. Season pork belly slices with salt and pepper. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Grill pork belly and chicken liver until well done. Pork skin should be crispy.
  3. Once grilled, chop the pork belly and chicken liver into small fine pieces.
  4. In a bowl, combine the chopped meat, onions, lemon juice and chili pepper. Season with soy sauce and pepper.
  5. Warm it in a pan with a bit of oil if desired. Alternatively, heat a sizzling plate and add a some butter or margarine. Add the Sisig and serve while still sizzling hot.
